# Project Charter

**Project Name:** Team Fhour  
**Project Sponsor:** Evan Holt & The Great Outdoors  

---

## 1. Project Purpose / Background

*(Why this project is being undertaken; business need or problem it addresses)*

- New hikers struggle to find accurate, centralized, and up-to-date trail information  
- Popular trails are overcrowded, while lesser-known trails are underused  
- Many websites are outdated, difficult to navigate, or focused mainly on advertising gear rather than safety and stewardship  

---

## 2. Project Objectives

*(What the project aims to achieve; should be specific and measurable if possible)*

- **Objective 1:** Provide reliable trail information  
- **Objective 2:** Promote safe hiking practices & seasonal awareness  
- **Objective 3:** Highlight lesser-known trails to reduce overcrowding  
- **Objective 4:** Build a community around hiking in the PNW  

### Success Criteria
- The website will be a trusted authority on PNW hiking  
- Build a community around hiking in the PNW  
- Successfully complete objectives  

---

## 3. Scope

### In Scope
- Trail reviews and rating  
- Guided hike registrations  
- Donation & membership support  

### Out of Scope
- Photo/video hosting  
- Hosting events  
- Interactive maps  

---

## 4. High-Level Deliverables

*(Key outputs/products of the project)*

- **Deliverable 1:** Documentation system for hiking trails  
- **Deliverable 2:** Donation system  
- **Deliverable 3:** User accounts  
- **Deliverable 4:** Visualizations

## 9. Assumptions and Constraints

### Assumptions
- Most relevant information can be stored in text form  
- Users will contribute updated information for free in exchange for community benefit  
- Project scope will be large enough to justify donations and partnerships  

### Constraints
- Low budget  
- Short timeline  
- Clients lacking technical experience rely on the dev team for implementation

## 10. Risks and Issues
