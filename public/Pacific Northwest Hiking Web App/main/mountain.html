Mountain

A mountain is a large landform that rises prominently above its surroundings, typically forming a distinct, elevated peak with steep sides. While there is no universally agreed-upon minimum height, geologists generally classify a landform as a mountain if it rises at least 1,000 feet (about 300 meters) above its base and features local, dramatic relief (sharp changes in elevation). Mountains rarely stand alone.They are usually found in clusters or long chains called mountain ranges, which are formed over millions of years by massive forces deep within the Earth.
