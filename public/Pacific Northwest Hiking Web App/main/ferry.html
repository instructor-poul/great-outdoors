Ferry

At its core, a ferry is a merchant vessel specifically designed to transport passengers, vehicles, and cargo across a body of water. Unlike cruise ships meant for long-distance leisure, ferries act as water-bound public transit, operating on fixed, frequent schedules to connect two or more shores over relatively short distances.
