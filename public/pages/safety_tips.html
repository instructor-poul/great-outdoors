Safety Tips

Important guidance to help you enjoy the outdoors safely and responsibly.

General Safety

Start with the basics to keep your outing calm and controlled.

Confirm whether you need a permit for your activity and carry it with you at all times.

Follow posted rules, stay on marked trails, and respect seasonal closures.

Keep children in sight and pets leashed whenever you are outdoors.

Wear protective clothing and sturdy hiking boots for uneven terrain.

Carry a compass and map so you can navigate when cell coverage is limited.

Emergency Preparation

Plan for unexpected situations with smart, compact gear.

Bring enough water and food for the planned trip plus a safety margin.

Pack a compact first-aid kit and know basic first-aid steps.

Share your route and return time with someone you trust.

Learn local emergency numbers and park contact details.

Consider using a whistle, headlamp, and portable charger.

Hydration

Keep your body fueled and alert by staying properly hydrated.

Carry at least 1–2 liters of water per person for short hikes; add more for hot or long trips.

Bring water purification tablets or a filter for overnight adventures.

Avoid alcohol as a hydration source — it increases dehydration risk.

Leave No Trace Principles

Protect the places you visit by leaving them as you found them.

Pack out everything you brought in and leave sites cleaner than you found them.

Do not remove plants, rocks, or cultural artifacts.

Use established campsites and avoid disturbing vegetation or streams.

Weather Awareness

Check conditions early and layer for comfort through changing weather.

Review weather forecasts before departing and plan accordingly.

Layer clothing so you can adapt to changing conditions.

Carry rain protection and be aware of heat, lightning, snow, or tidal hazards.

Wildlife Safety

Respect wildlife by observing from a distance and securing your scent.

Store food and scented items securely, using bear-resistant containers where required.

Observe wildlife from a distance and never approach or feed animals.

Carry bear spray in bear country and know how to use it.

Inspect gear and clothing for pests before leaving natural areas.
