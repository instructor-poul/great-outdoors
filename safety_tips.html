Safety Tips

  • Confirm whether you will need a permit for your chosen activity, and carry it with you at all times.
  • Follow all posted rules.
  • Keep children in sight and pets leashed at all times.
  • Wear protective clothing and hiking boots.
  • Carry a compass and a map for reliable navigation outside cell coverage.
  • Stay on marked trails.

Emergency Preparation

  • Ensure you have a full tank of gas, plentiful drinking water, and sufficient food to last the trip.
  • Pack a first aid kit, and be sure you know how to use it.
  • Make sure someone knows where you are going and when you mean to return, and keep to that plan.
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency contact information, both for yourself and the park you are visiting.
  • Keep in mind the cell coverage can be limited in remote areas. If your phone has an automated emergency notification function, familiarize yourself with its particulars.
  • Consider hiking with a buddy.
  • When visiting coastal areas, be aware of tsunami evacuation procedures.

Hydration

  • Pack bottled water or use purification tablets to avoid waterborne pathogens.
  • Do not pack alcohol as a substitute for drinking water, and check local regulations.
  • Pay attention to posted or online swimming warnings. When in doubt, refrain from swimming.

Leave No Trace Principles

  • Dispose of garbage in designated receptacles, and be ready to pack out any food wrappers or garbage containers.
  • Refrain from taking souvenirs such as plants, rocks, driftwood or antlers.
  • Don't clean dishes or laundry in natural water.

Weather Awareness

  • Check online or print resources for your destination for specific weather or seasonal hazards. If you need more information, call ahead.
  • Bring or wear clothing appropriate to seasonal and weather conditions.
  • Never park on dry grass, the heat from cars can start fires.
  • Exercise caution when driving in icy conditions, and use snow tires or tire chains where recommended.

Wildlife Safety

  • Keep food, food waste, and anything with a scent in a vehicle, animal-proof container, or hang.
  • Refrain from feeding, harassing, or petting wild animals.
  • Check accessories and gear for plants, mud, and insects at any life stage, and don't transport firewood from elsewhere.
  • Wash your vehicle before returning home to prevent invasive species from travelling with you.
  • Avoid hiking around dawn and dusk hours.
  • Carry bear spray while travelling in bear habitats.
  • Maintain minimum distance from wildlife: 30m from ungulates and 100m from predators.
