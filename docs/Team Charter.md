
# Team Purpose and Mission

## Purpose / Mission Statement:
(Why this team exists and what it aims to achieve)

Our Team will collaboratively design, build, and deliver the Pacific Northwest Hiking Web App for our client. We aim to apply our development skills in a real-world setting, produce a functional and polished product, and grow as engineers through teamwork and shared accountability.

 

## Team Goals:

(Short-term and long-term goals, aligned with project success)

**Short-term:** Deliver a working MVP of the Pacific Northwest Hiking Web App – including trail search, filtering, and map display – by the end of the project sprint

**Long-term:** Build team capability to design and ship impactful web products efficiently, and establish practices (code review, testing, documentation) that scale to future projects

## Team Norms and Operating Guidelines

### Meeting Cadence:
**Frequency:** Weekly or Bi-Weekly – every Tuesday afternoon
**Duration / Format:** 60 minutes, meeting in Library or via Discord video call

### Communication Guidelines:

**Primary tools:** Discord (daily chat, announcements, and video calls)

**Response time expectations:** Respond to Discord messages within 12 hours on weekdays; acknowledge urgent items within 6 hours during daytime

**File sharing / documentation:** GitHub for all code; shared Discord Channel for meeting notes, design documents, and deliverables

### Behavioral Norms / Expectations:

**Attendance:** All members are expected at every weekly meeting; notify the team on Discord at least 2 hours in advance if unable to attend

**Participation:** Every member actively contributes to discussions, completes assigned tasks on time, and provides progress updates at each meeting

**Accountability / quality standards:** All code must pass peer review (at least one reviewer) before merging.  Deliverables must be tested and documented before submission

### Decision-Making Process

**How decisions will be made:** Consensus is the default option. If consensus cannot be reached, the team moves to a majority vote

**Escalation process:** If a majority vote is tied or unresolvable, the Lead Developer makes the final call.  Persistent disagreements are escalated to the course instructor

## Conflict Resolution Plan

### Steps to resolve conflicts within the team:
**Step 1** – Direct conversation: The individuals involved address the issue privately and respectfully before involving the team

**Step 2** – Team discussion: If unresolved, the conflict is raised at the next team meeting for open, solution-focused dialogue

**Step 3** – Mediated resolution: The Lead Developer facilitates a structured discussion to identify a fair outcome acceptable to all parties

**Step 4** – Documented agreement: The resolution is written down in Discord or a shared document and shared with the full team

**Step 5** – Additional steps as needed, maintaining respectful and solution-focused dialogue throughout

**Escalate to team lead or sponsor if unresolved:** If the conflict persists after team-level mediation, the issue is escalated to the course instructor for final resolution
